How SS and ROP are readjusted

The statistical baseline. Safety stock buffers demand variability over lead time at a chosen service level; reorder point is average lead-time demand plus that buffer:

SS = Z × σd × √LT
ROP = (d̄ × LT) + SS

Z: 98% → 2.05 · 95% → 1.65 · 90% → 1.28 · σd = std-dev of period demand · d̄ = mean demand · LT = lead time.

The rule that matters: with zero trailing 12-month consumption, d̄ = 0 and σd = 0 — so SS = 0 and ROP = 0 by the math itself. Remaining safety stock on these materials is a legacy setting, not a calculation.

Worked example — SP-4415 hydraulic seal kit:

Old (set 2022): SS 40 / ROP 65 · 58 on hand at $86 → $4,988 locked, ~$1,097/yr carrying.

Trailing 12-month demand: 0 — machine decommissioned in the 2024 line upgrade.

Recalc: d̄ = 0 → SS 0, ROP 0. Keep 2 insurance spares under criticality policy; liquidate 56 → recover ~30¢/$, release ~$1,440 cash + ~$1,000/yr.

Insurance spares are the honest exception: zero-demand ≠ zero-need for single-point-of-failure parts. Gate via engineering criticality review, never the forecast formula.

Reducing stocking cost at scale — Celonis and Aera

Celonis — find why excess stock keeps being created

Process mining · diagnosis layer
  • Mines actual ERP event logs (P2P, inventory movements) — how stock really flows, not how the SOP says.
  • Root causes: duplicate material numbers, maverick buying, POs raised despite stock, SS/ROP untouched for years.
  • Working-capital apps quantify cash release per material/plant where days-on-hand and parameters diverge.
  • Continuous monitoring: alerts when a buyer re-creates the dead-stock pattern.

Vendor cases typically report 10–20% excess-inventory reduction; treat published figures as directional.

Aera — decide and execute the parameter changes

Decision intelligence · action layer
  • Aera Decision Cloud recalculates SS/ROP per SKU-location continuously from live demand and lead times.
  • Dollar-scored recommendations with rationale, routed for approval.
  • Closed loop: approved changes write back to ERP MRP views automatically — the step most programs never finish.
  • Learns from planner accept/override decisions.

Pattern to copy even without either tool: diagnose root causes, automate recalculation, gate with human approval, write back.

90-day action playbook
  1. Extract & verify (wks 1–2): 24 months of goods issues per material/plant; flag zero-consumption ≥ 12 months; verify against open reservations and BOM usage.
  2. Segment (wks 2–4): insurance spares (engineering review) · project residuals (return/restock) · duplicates (merge masters) · true dead stock (liquidate/scrap with finance approval).
  3. Reset parameters (wks 4–6): SS/ROP = 0 on zero-demand; MTO or 98% → 90% service level on sporadic (~37% SS cut); document every change.
  4. Recover cash (wks 6–12): internal transfers → vendor buy-backs → surplus brokers → scrap last, with write-off approvals.
  5. Prevent recurrence: monthly zero-consumption report, PO block on flagged materials, quarterly recalculation — manually or via the Celonis → Aera pattern.
