    Use cases UC-1…UC-5

    UC-1
    Victim self-service intake

    A victim organization captures incident facts in a structured, IC3-aligned form immediately after discovery, while details are fresh — no backend, no account, no data leaves the browser.

    UC-2
    Deterministic triage

    A transparent scoring engine (every point traceable to a factor) assigns CRITICAL/HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW so IR leads can prioritize. Reproducible math — suitable as QMS/audit evidence; no LLM in the numeric path.

    UC-3
    Law-enforcement reporting handoff

    One click saves the validated, triaged JSON record to the local drive — an evidence pack ready to reference when contacting the local FBI field office and filing at ic3.gov, where complaints are reviewed and may be forwarded to law enforcement or partner agencies.

    UC-4
    Local incident register

    Confirmed incidents persist in IndexedDB (the free DB shipped with every browser) as an exportable register for post-incident review, insurers, and regulators.

    UC-5
    Tabletop training

    Three preloaded exfiltration scenarios animate the full pipeline for exercises and demos — displayed only, never written to the store.

    Architecture static SPA on EC2

    Trust boundary: everything numeric and persistent happens inside the victim's browser; the EC2 instance only serves the static file and never receives incident data. There is no network egress path at all; records are saved to the operator's local drive as JSON. This keeps the hosting tier out of scope for breach-notification analysis on the tool itself.

    OV-1 — High-level operational concept DoDAF-style

    OV-1 reading: the adversary exfiltrates and extorts (red, dashed); the victim's SOC/IR team uses the Incident Reporter to turn raw incident facts into a validated, triaged JSON record; that record backs the operator-initiated flows (cyan) to the local FBI field office and IC3, with conditional flows (grey) to CISA, IR counsel, insurer, and regulators when exfiltration triggers notification duties.

    BitSense demo tool · single-file HTML · vanilla JS · IndexedDB persistence · no external dependencies · not an official FBI/IC3 submission channel — always file at ic3.gov.
